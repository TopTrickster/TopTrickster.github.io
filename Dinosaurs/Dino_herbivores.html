DINOSAURS

Herbivores

Information:
Herbivorous dinosaurs (Herbivores) were plant-eating dinosaurs that thrived from the late Triassic to the end of the Cretaceous period. These dinosaurs had a range of adaptations for consuming vegetation, such as flat teeth for grinding leaves and specialized beaks for stripping plants. They were often large and had diverse body shapes, from the long-necked Brachiosaurus, which could reach high vegetation, to the armored Stegosaurus, with its distinctive plates and spikes. Herbivorous dinosaurs played a vital role in their ecosystems by consuming vast amounts of plant material and serving as prey for carnivorous dinosaurs. Their varied feeding strategies and physical adaptations allowed them to thrive in different environments.
