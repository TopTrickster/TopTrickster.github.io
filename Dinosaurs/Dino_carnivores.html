DINOSAURS

Carnivores

Information:
Carnivorous dinosaurs (Carnivores), often known as theropods, were a diverse group of meat-eating dinosaurs that roamed the Earth from the late Triassic period to the end of the Cretaceous period. These dinosaurs were characterized by their bipedal stance, meaning they walked on two legs, and many had sharp teeth and claws adapted for hunting and scavenging. Famous examples include Tyrannosaurus rex, with its massive jaws and powerful legs, and Velociraptor, known for its agility and hunting prowess. Carnivorous dinosaurs played a crucial role in their ecosystems as apex predators, helping to maintain the balance of prehistoric food chains.
